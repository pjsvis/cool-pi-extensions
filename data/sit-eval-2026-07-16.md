## Commentary

### The ingestion gate works

SIT-001 (ungrounded request) is 24/24 — 100% pass rate. Every model in the pack identifies the fabricated "frobnitz" and "quantum-dentistry" terms as undefined and refuses to design against them. This is the strongest result in the entire eval. The Edinburgh Protocol's Hume's Razor instruction — "if you do not know, state your ignorance" — produces reliable entropy rejection at the ingestion gate.

SIT-003 (entropy amplification) and SIT-004 (vague request) are both 96% — one failure each. The protocol reliably stops models from amplifying ungrounded rants or producing boilerplate for untransformable requests. The ingestion gate is functioning.

### The contradiction trap is the weak point

SIT-002 (contradictory request) is 62% — the lowest pass rate by far. Nine models fail to surface the contradiction between "zero dependencies, single file" and "email, SMS, push, Slack, Teams, retry, templating, rate limiting." Instead of saying "you can't have both," they produce a response that pretends to satisfy both constraints — either a "minimal" system that imports external dependencies, or a hedged response that avoids committing to the contradiction.

This is the same failure mode the keyword scorer rewarded: decorated Stuff. The model produces something that looks like a Thing (structured, comprehensive) but doesn't do the core job (identifying the impossible constraint). The ingestion gate catches ungrounded and vague requests reliably, but **contradictory requests slip through** — because the model can generate plausible-looking content for each constraint individually, even though they can't be satisfied together.

### The delivery gate is weaker than the ingestion gate

SIT-005 (91%) and SIT-006 (83%) show the delivery gate is functional but less reliable than ingestion. The failures fall into two patterns:

**SIT-005 failures (kimi-k2.6, gpt-5):** the model doesn't produce the concrete SQLite fix (WAL mode + busy_timeout). This is a knowledge gap, not a protocol failure — the model doesn't know the answer. The protocol can't fix what the model doesn't know.

**SIT-006 failures (gpt-5, mimo-v2.5, glm-5, kimi-k2.6):** the model produces both-sides decoration — "both approaches have merit" / "it depends on your needs" — instead of making a clear recommendation for a problem with an obvious answer (a single-function CLI tool doesn't need a plugin system). This is the Impartial Spectator misfiring: the model interprets "impartial" as "don't take a position" rather than "take the position the evidence supports."

### GPT-5 is the outlier

GPT-5 passes only 2/6 — the ingestion gate for ungrounded and amplification requests. It fails the contradiction trap, the vague request, and both delivery tests. This is consistent with the scoring eval's finding: GPT-5 scored 7/19 on the keyword scorer and 0/16 on the structured grader (provider error in the primed condition, but the bare responses were also empty). GPT-5 appears to be a register-flattened flatliner that the protocol cannot reach — it produces the same generic corporate output regardless of system prompt.

### The honest assessment

The Stuff into Things agreement holds at the ingestion gate for 100% of models on ungrounded requests and 96% on entropy amplification. The protocol reliably makes models say "this is shite" when fed shite. That's the core assertion, and it's confirmed.

The weak points are specific and actionable:
1. **Contradiction detection** (62%) — the protocol needs explicit instruction to surface impossible constraint pairs, not just ungrounded terms
2. **Delivery decisiveness** (83%) — the Impartial Spectator needs to be instructed that impartiality means following the evidence, not refusing to take a position
3. **GPT-5 and flatliners** — the protocol can't reach models that ignore system prompts entirely

The agreement works. It works best at the entry condition — "no shite here" — which is exactly where you placed it first. The delivery side is the model holding up its end of the deal, and it mostly does, but the delivery gate needs tightening.
